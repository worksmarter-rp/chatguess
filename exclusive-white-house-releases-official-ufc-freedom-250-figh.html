The White House Press Office released the complete fight card for UFC Freedom 250 Tuesday evening, confirming President Biden will face former President Trump in the main event on the South Lawn this Saturday. The leaked document, stamped by the Bureau of Executive Athletic Coordination, lists official fighter statistics including Biden's 6'0" reach advantage and Trump's 12-pound weight edge in the heavyweight division.

"This represents a historic opportunity for American democracy to settle differences through regulated combat sports," said an unnamed official from the newly formed Department of Political Pugilism. The bout will be officiated by Chief Justice John Roberts, who completed his referee certification through a weekend course at the UFC Performance Institute last month.

The undercard features Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer facing Minority Leader Mitch McConnell in what promoters are calling the "Filibuster Finale." McConnell enters with an undefeated 287-0 record, though critics note 284 of those victories came by way of procedural knockout. The fight contract specifies a 60-vote threshold for victory, with ties going to the parliamentarian.

Justice Clarence Thomas is listed as the early favorite at -150 odds, citing his extensive experience with undisclosed conflicts of interest as superior training for combat sports.

The preliminary card includes a tag-team match between the House Freedom Caucus and the Congressional Progressive Caucus, though organizers noted difficulty in determining which members actually belong to each group. Speaker Mike Johnson will serve as special guest timekeeper, armed with his signature gavel and a backup constitutional amendment.

According to internal budget documents, the event's $700,000 lawn restoration fee is listed as a standard line item between fighter purses and medical testing. The White House Grounds Department has reportedly ordered 50 tons of specialized combat-grade grass seed rated for heavyweight political impact.

Pay-per-view revenue will fund the national debt, with C-SPAN providing exclusive coverage. The broadcast team includes Joe Rogan and Wolf Blitzer, who sources say have been practicing their "democracy is in the octagon tonight" catchphrases since last Tuesday.

First Lady Jill Biden will present the winner with a custom championship belt featuring the presidential seal and 50 stars representing each state's individual contribution to the spectacle. The ceremony is scheduled immediately following the post-fight press conference and drug testing.
