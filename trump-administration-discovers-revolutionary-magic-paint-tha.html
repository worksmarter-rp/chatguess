Trump Administration Discovers Revolutionary 'Magic Paint' That Experts Say Doesn't Work on Buildings

Preservationists warn silicate-based miracle coating would permanently damage historic granite, but hey, it's magic.

By Staff Reporter, Bureau of Government Affairs
APR 14, 2026  ·  GOVMENT.ORG

Govment.org photo illustration  |  APR 14, 2026

The Trump administration announced Tuesday its intention to apply a revolutionary "magic paint" coating to the historic Eisenhower Executive Office Building, despite unanimous expert consensus that the silicate-based substance is fundamentally incompatible with the structure's granite exterior.

According to internal memos obtained by the Bureau of Executive Facility Enhancement, the paint—officially designated as "Revolutionary Coating Solution Alpha-7"—has been described by administration officials as possessing "unprecedented magical properties" that will "transform government efficiency through enhanced building aesthetics."

Dr. Margaret Chen, a preservation specialist with the National Trust for Historic Preservation, explained that silicate-based coatings create irreversible chemical bonds with limestone and concrete but cannot penetrate granite's crystalline structure. "It's like trying to glue water to a mirror," Chen said. "The molecular composition makes adherence physically impossible, regardless of any purported magical qualities."

The Eisenhower Executive Office Building, completed in 1888, features walls constructed entirely of Maine granite quarried specifically for its durability and weather resistance. Historical records indicate the building has withstood 136 years of Washington weather without requiring exterior coating of any kind.

"We believe this magic paint represents a paradigm shift in federal building maintenance protocols," said Deputy Director of Facilities Management Bradley Hutchinson. "Previous administrations were constrained by so-called scientific limitations."

The Bureau of Magical Building Solutions has reportedly allocated $4.7 million for the coating project, despite receiving written warnings from seventeen preservation organizations, three chemistry departments, and the building's original architectural firm, which still maintains offices in Boston.

Installation crews have been instructed to begin surface preparation next month, focusing initially on the building's north facade. Officials declined to specify what happens if the magic paint fails to adhere to the granite as predicted.

The Eisenhower building houses approximately 550 executive branch staff members who work daily in offices overlooking the White House South Lawn.
