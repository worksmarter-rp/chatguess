The White House Office of Presidential Communications released the complete fight card for tonight's UFC Freedom 250 event, scheduled to begin at 8 p.m. on the South Lawn following standard Secret Service octagon perimeter protocols. The event, sanctioned by the newly established Federal Mixed Martial Arts Commission, represents what officials describe as "the next logical evolution of American democratic participation."

The main event features the highly anticipated Trump-Biden rematch, with both fighters having completed mandatory drug testing through the Bureau of Executive Athletic Performance. Trump enters with a 1-0 record following his 2016 victory by split decision, while Biden maintains a 1-1 professional record after his 2020 technical knockout victory and a controversial loss to inflation metrics in early 2022. The bout will be officiated by Chief Justice John Roberts, who received emergency referee certification from the Nevada State Athletic Commission last Thursday.

The co-main event showcases the Nine-Justice Battle Royal, featuring all Supreme Court justices competing simultaneously in a steel cage match to determine constitutional interpretation privileges for the 2024-2025 term. Justice Clarence Thomas enters as the betting favorite at -200, having never lost a case involving RV maintenance or luxury vacation planning. The Bureau of Judicial Athletics confirmed that Justice Amy Coney Barrett will compete despite a minor gavel-related wrist injury sustained during oral arguments last week.

Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell headlines the preliminary card, defending his unprecedented 287-0 professional record built entirely through technical decisions and opponent disqualifications. McConnell faces freshman Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez in what analysts predict will be a classic striker-versus-grappler matchup. The Kentucky senator's signature "Procedural Stall" submission hold has never been successfully defended, according to Congressional Athletic Commission records.

The undercard features the Senate Budget Committee in their 47th consecutive bout against actual numbers, maintaining their winless streak dating back to fiscal year 1969. Committee Chairman Sheldon Whitehouse expressed confidence despite the historical precedent, telling reporters that "numbers have never faced our new deficit-neutral uppercut combination." The Bureau of Mathematical Combat Sports rates the Committee as a 50,000-to-1 underdog.

The restoration includes specialized octagon-rated grass capable of withstanding repeated democratic impact and blood-resistant fertilizer approved by both the EPA and the Federal Election Commission.

White House Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre noted during the pre-fight press conference that all participants signed standard liability waivers and agreed to binding arbitration through the Supreme Court, pending the outcome of tonight's Nine-Justice Battle Royal. Tickets remain available for $2,024 per seat, with proceeds benefiting the Presidential Campaign Finance Reform Through Combat Sports Initiative.

The event will be broadcast live on C-SPAN, with Spanish-language commentary provided by the Bureau of Multilingual Democratic Participation.
