For fourteen years, the Wentworth Institute has asked one question: what separates the leaders who thrive from the leaders who are merely tolerated? The answer, distilled across 200 Fortune 500 engagements and one very productive weekend in Scottsdale, is VELMA.

What critics call “dysfunction,” we call a framework. And we have sold it back to their employers for $4,800 per cohort.

The Five Pillars

Each validated through proprietary research  ·  Not peer-reviewed in any traditional sense

V Visionary Opacity “Direction without distraction”

High-performing leaders communicate strategy at an altitude where it cannot be questioned, measured, or proven wrong.

  • Strategy too broad to be wrong
  • All-hands answers no questions
  • Calls Q4 targets “learnings in motion”
E Empowerment Theater “Autonomy with guardrails”

Teams perform measurably better when they believe they have ownership of a decision, regardless of whether they do.

  • Asks opinions post-decision
  • Delegates work, retains credit
  • “Your call” said in a tone that isn’t
L Leveraged Meeting Culture “Collaboration at scale”

A decision made outside a meeting is a decision that lacks alignment, and a decision that lacks alignment is an opportunity for a meeting.

  • Schedules meetings to plan meetings
  • 14 attendees, for “visibility”
  • Recaps hour one during hour two
M Managed Accountability “Consequences as a last resort”

Rather than holding individuals accountable for outcomes — which research shows creates discomfort — VELMA organizations hold outcomes accountable for individuals, and coach from there.

  • Failure reframed as “a data point”
  • Moves the deadline, not the person
  • PIP delivered after the farewell party
A Agile Rebranding “Continuous organizational evolution”

The most adaptive organizations treat their own structure as a product in perpetual beta, ensuring no one is encumbered by the institutional memory of how things used to work.

  • Reorgs every 11–14 months
  • New framework before old one lands
  • Layoffs called “right-sizing toward growth”
